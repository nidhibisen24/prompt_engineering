Chain of Thought (CoT)

Chain-of-Thought prompting enables Large Language Models to tackle complex reasoning tasks by breaking them down into intermediate steps. Instead of jumping straight to an answer, the model "thinks out loud."

The Difference

Standard Prompting

"The group has 10 apples. They use 2 to make pie and buy 5 more. How many apples are there?"

Output:

The answer is 13.

CoT Prompting

"...Let's think step by step."

Output:

1. Start with 10 apples.
2. Subtract 2 (pie) = 8 apples.
3. Add 5 (bought) = 13 apples.
The answer is 13.

How to Implement CoT

1

Zero-Shot Trigger

Simply add "Let's think step by step" to your prompt. This triggers the model's reasoning sequence.

2

Manual Few-Shot CoT

Provide 2-3 examples where you manually write out the reasoning steps before the final answer.

3

Self-Consistency

Ask the model to generate multiple paths of reasoning and choose the most frequent answer.

Pro Tip

Chain of Thought is most effective for models with 10B+ parameters. Smaller models may struggle to maintain logic throughout the "chain."
